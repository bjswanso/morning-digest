Personal Care

**1. Physical Health — Box Breathing (5 minutes)**

Before your first meeting of the week, try box breathing: inhale for 4 counts, hold for 4, exhale for 4, hold for 4. Repeat 4–5 times. It measurably lowers cortisol and activates the parasympathetic nervous system — think of it as a firmware reset for your nervous system before the Monday queue loads. No app required.

**2. Gratitude — The Specificity Upgrade (5 minutes)**

Vague gratitude ("I'm grateful for my health") runs on autopilot and loses its effect quickly. Today, write down one thing you're grateful for and force yourself to name the exact mechanism — *why* it matters, *how* it came to be, *who* made it possible. Specificity activates the brain's reward circuitry more powerfully than generic reflection and, as a bonus, primes an INTJ's favorite organ: the analytical mind.

**3. Relationship — The Two-Question Check-In (10 minutes)**

Tonight, ask your spouse two questions: "What's one thing on your mind that I don't know about?" and "Is there anything I can do this week that would make your life easier?" Then listen without problem-solving. Research on relationship quality consistently shows that feeling *heard* outranks feeling *advised* as a predictor of satisfaction. You're a senior manager — you already know active listening is a competitive advantage. Apply it at home too.
