Section 2 — Personal Care

**1. Physical Health — 5-minute desk reset (≤5 min)**

Your posture right now is almost certainly a forward-head, shoulders-rolled hypothesis test that your body keeps confirming. Spend 3–5 minutes on a targeted thoracic mobility drill: sit at the edge of your chair, interlace fingers behind your head, and gently extend your upper back over the chair back. Ten slow reps. This counteracts the compressive load of screen work and — unlike the meeting you just left — produces measurable results.

**2. Strengthen a Relationship with Your Spouse — Micro-appreciation ritual (≤5 min)**

Text or tell your spouse one specific, concrete thing they did in the last 48 hours that made your life easier or better. Not a generic "thanks for everything" — a named behavior: *the coffee you made before I had my 7 a.m. call*, *the way you handled the scheduling chaos on Saturday*. Specificity is the difference between a courtesy and a deposit. Research consistently links expressed specific gratitude to higher relationship satisfaction. Set a recurring Monday-morning reminder so it becomes a system, not a mood.

**3. Gratitude — The "contrast effect" journal prompt (≤10 min)**

Positive psychology research finds that imagining the absence of something good — rather than simply listing what you're grateful for — produces a stronger and more lasting emotional response. Pick one thing you value (a skill, a relationship, a circumstance) and spend 5–7 minutes writing about what your life would look like without it. The exercise essentially runs a counterfactual simulation, and your brain, being the pattern-recognition engine it is, updates its appreciation accordingly. No ritual required — this works equally well in a notes app at 6 a.m.
