Section 2 — Personal Care

**1. 🧠 Mental Health — The 2-Minute Rule for Cognitive Decompression**

INTJs running on parallel processing tracks all day (medtech ops *and* AI coursework — the strategic equivalent of running two operating systems at once) are particularly prone to decision fatigue by midday. Try the "2-minute brain dump" right now: set a timer, write every open loop in your head — tasks, worries, half-formed ideas — onto paper or a notes app. No organizing, just emptying. Research on cognitive offloading shows that externalizing mental content reduces working memory load and measurably lowers anxiety. Once it's on paper, your brain stops treating it like urgent RAM that can't be freed.

**2. 🤝 Strengthening Your Relationship — The Specific Appreciation Micro-Ritual**

General gratitude ("thanks for everything") is the verbal equivalent of a form letter. Specificity is what lands. Before you move to your next task today, send your spouse one message naming a concrete thing they did in the last 48 hours and exactly why it mattered to you. ("When you handled X so I didn't have to think about it, I felt genuinely supported" hits differently than "you're great.") Relationship researchers call this "perceived partner responsiveness" — and it's one of the strongest predictors of long-term relationship satisfaction. Takes 90 seconds. Return on investment: disproportionately high.

**3. 🌿 Physical Health — The 10-Minute Walk That Earns Compound Interest**

One study published in *Nature Medicine* found that just 11 minutes of brisk walking per day was associated with significantly lower risk of cardiovascular disease and certain cancers. You don't need the gym; you need a block and your shoes. Step away from the screen now — not after this next email, now — and walk briskly for 10 minutes. It also resets cortisol levels, which is the closest thing to a biological "ctrl+alt+delete" available without a prescription. Bonus: if you're in Phoenix today, go before 9 AM or after 7 PM, because a 108°F high is its own kind of data point.
