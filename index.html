Personal Care

**1. Physical Health — 10-Minute Micro-Walk**

Step outside for exactly 10 minutes before you open your first meeting of the day. Walk at a pace where you could talk but wouldn't want to. The research on brief post-waking movement (even 10 minutes) shows measurable improvements in executive function and mood regulation for the following two hours — i.e., the hours when your calendar is already full. Think of it as defragging your prefrontal cortex before anyone else gets to use it.

**2. Relationship — The Specificity Compliment**

At some point today, tell your spouse one specific thing you noticed them do recently — not a generality ("you're so supportive"), but a scene ("I noticed you checked in on me last night after my study session, and I felt seen"). Specificity is the difference between a compliment and evidence. It takes 90 seconds and has a disproportionate return on investment.

**3. Gratitude — The Constraint Reframe**

Take 5 minutes to write down one constraint in your life right now (time, energy, bandwidth) and then list two things that constraint has unexpectedly *given* you — a skill you built, a priority you clarified, a habit you formed. This is the INTJ version of a gratitude journal: not "I'm thankful for sunsets," but "this bottleneck made me a better systems thinker." Turns out constraint is just optimization with worse PR.
