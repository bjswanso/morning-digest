Section 2 — Personal Care

**1. Physical Health — 5-Minute Morning Activation (Not a Workout, a Recalibration)**

Before opening a single app, spend five minutes doing joint mobility circles — ankles, hips, shoulders, neck — in sequence. Think of it as rebooting the hardware before loading the software. At 50, your connective tissue appreciates the memo that the day has begun. Research consistently links brief morning movement to improved energy regulation and reduced cortisol spikes — exactly what you don't need going into a 9 a.m. leadership call.

**2. Gratitude — The Specificity Rule**

Write down one thing you're grateful for, but ban the generic ("my health," "my family"). Force yourself to be *precise*: a single interaction, a sentence from last night's reading, the exact moment something clicked. The science on gratitude journaling shows that specificity, not volume, drives the mood benefit — your brain engages more deeply when it has to retrieve a real detail rather than reach for a placeholder. One sentence. Clock it at under two minutes.

**3. Strengthening a Relationship with Your Spouse — The 6-Second Rule**

Before you both scatter into your respective days, pause for a genuine six-second hug or greeting. This isn't sentimentality for its own sake: relationship researcher John Gottman's work identifies the six-second connection as long enough to shift neurochemistry — oxytocin rises, cortisol drops, and both parties carry a baseline of goodwill into whatever the day deploys at them. For two people running at full throttle, it's the highest-ROI six seconds available.
